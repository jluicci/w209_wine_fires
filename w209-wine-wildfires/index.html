How do wildfires impact the California wine industry?

This website is an exploration of historical wildfire trends, air quality, and grape production in California.

Our team chose this topic in light of the devastating 2020 wildfire season in California and the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. Because wildfires struck right before and during the August-October harvest season, many vineyards were left with smoke-tainted grapes that were unusable for wine production. Our goal is to create insights for vinters and wine enthusiasts who seek to analyze trends in wildfires, air quality and grape production throughout the past decade.

How to Use this Website

  • 1

    Explore trends in wildfires over time

    Compare by county or fire origin date to understand how individual fires affected different regions and how many acres were burned in California.

  • 2

    Analyze the impact on air quality by county and quarter

    Poor air quality over an extended period of time could indicate grapes in the region were susceptible to smoke taint. Wind patterns can carry wildfire smoke to disparate areas, also posing a risk to regions that may not have a fire nearby.

  • 3

    Find corresponding grape production levels by grape pricing region

    Consider whether grape production in a county's corresponding grape pricing region differed from the average. Keep in mind, there are still other factors not accounted for that can play a role in grape production volumes.

Air Quality

Overview of wildfire impact on AQI by county: Wildfires are highlighted in bright red with the air quality index (AQI) represented by shading. Darker shaded regions represent counties with a higher AQI, which indicates more particles in the air and poorer quality air. Adjust the date range selector to hone in on a specific time frame of interest. This presents a complementary geographic view of air quality index by county in relation to the acres burned in the prior visual.

Individual County AQI trend explorer: Select a county and date range to view the AQI over time and the aggregate number of days the AQI was in acceptable or unacceptable ranges. This same county and date range filter will also apply to the AQI Daily Measurements scatter plot, which uses the same color scale for AQI ranges to represent AQI by day.

Conclusion

Using a combination of wildfire and air quality index mapping, users can roughly estimate whether a wine-producing region was susceptible to fire impact, either through physical loss or smoke-tainted grapes. If it was physical loss, users can then confirm whether grape production in that county's corresponding grape pricing region was lower than average.

Smoke-taint, however, is harder to predict. Researchers and vinters are still trying to understand the specific conditions that lead to smoke taint. Additionally, it is difficult to attribute a decrease in grape production specifically to a fire, as other conditions play a key role, particularly drought.

On the bright side, even though wine makers typically won't use smoke tainted grapes, some distilleries are now making vodka from these grapes!
